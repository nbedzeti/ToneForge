# How to Install Your Custom Ringtone

After creating your M4R ringtone file in RingToneMaker, follow these steps to install it on your iPhone:

## Method 1: Using Files App (Recommended)

1. **Share the Ringtone**
   - Tap "Share as Ringtone" in the app
   - Select "Save to Files"
   - Choose a location (iCloud Drive or On My iPhone)

2. **Access via Files App**
   - Open the Files app on your iPhone
   - Navigate to where you saved the M4R file
   - The file is now accessible for other apps

3. **Install via Computer** (Required for actual ringtone installation)
   - Connect your iPhone to your Mac or PC
   - On Mac: Open Finder
   - On PC: Open iTunes
   - Drag the M4R file from Files app to your computer
   - Then sync it back to your iPhone's Tones section

## Method 2: Using AirDrop to Mac

1. **Share via AirDrop**
   - Tap "Share as Ringtone" in the app
   - Select your Mac from AirDrop options
   - Accept the file on your Mac

2. **Add to iPhone**
   - On Mac: Open Finder
   - Connect your iPhone
   - Drag the M4R file to the Tones section
   - Sync your iPhone

## Method 3: Using Email/Messages

1. **Share via Email or Messages**
   - Tap "Share as Ringtone"
   - Choose Mail or Messages
   - Send to yourself or another device

2. **Download and Install**
   - Open the email/message on your computer
   - Download the M4R file
   - Use Finder (Mac) or iTunes (PC) to sync to iPhone

## Setting as Ringtone

Once the M4R file is synced to your iPhone:

1. Open **Settings** app
2. Go to **Sounds & Haptics**
3. Tap **Ringtone**
4. Your custom ringtone will appear at the top of the list
5. Select it to set as your ringtone

## Important Notes

- **iOS Limitation**: Due to iOS security restrictions, ringtones cannot be installed directly from third-party apps
- **Computer Required**: You need a Mac or PC with Finder/iTunes to complete the installation
- **File Format**: The M4R format is specifically designed for iPhone ringtones
- **Duration**: Ringtones are automatically limited to 30 seconds
- **GarageBand Alternative**: You can also import M4R files into GarageBand and share them as ringtones from there

## Troubleshooting

**Ringtone doesn't appear in Settings:**
- Make sure the file is in M4R format (not M4A)
- Verify the file was synced to the Tones section, not Music
- Try restarting your iPhone after syncing

**Can't sync to iPhone:**
- Ensure your iPhone is trusted on your computer
- Check that you have the latest version of macOS/iTunes
- Try using a different USB cable

**File won't open:**
- Verify the file extension is .m4r
- Check that the file isn't corrupted (try creating a new one)
- Ensure the duration is 30 seconds or less

## Alternative: GarageBand Method

If you have GarageBand installed:

1. Share the M4R file to GarageBand
2. Import it as an audio file
3. Trim if needed (though it should already be trimmed)
4. Tap the share icon
5. Select "Ringtone"
6. Name it and export
7. It will appear directly in Settings > Sounds & Haptics

This method bypasses the need for a computer but requires GarageBand to be installed.
